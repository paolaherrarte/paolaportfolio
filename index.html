About Me

Hi, and welcome to my personal page! I'm happy you're here! I've created this page to showcase my projects, that I am incredibly proud of, and to have a place where I can see myself grow. As I continue on this path, I can only think of how far I've come since I wrote my first line of code. It was the summer of 2019, and aimlessly scrolling through TikTok when I saw an influencer coding. I thought, "Wow! That person must be very smart cause that all looks super confusing!" I can't say that after that 30 second TikTok I immediately started to code, actually it was a couple of months later, I saw yet another coding TikTok on my feed and then I began to research and see what this was all about. I had no idea, what HTML was, much less CSS. But I continued to learn and search into this amazing career that felt like a whole new world for me. Finally, after much researching, I downloaded an IDE, Pycharm, and wrote my first line of code in Python.

print('Hello World')

After that day, I was hooked. I couldn't stop researching, I read article after article. I was convinced that this was the career for me and planned to go to a coding bootcamp, Flatiron, to be exact. I intended to attend the school in Washington, DC. All these thoughts and decisions were at the end of 2019 and beginning of 2020. Unfortunately, COVID-19 hit and as the world closed down, my family and I had to move to another state. My goals of learning how to code were put on hold. After about two years, I finally was told about a wonderful online bootcamp called Generation USA. I applied, and was elected for a cohort! I was over the moon, I was super excited to embark in something that I had waited so long to do. My previous experience before Generation was only Python, but the bootcamp was front-end web development. There were many obstacles, to started my specific cohort was 7-10pm MST, I live on the east coast, so class for me was from 9-12am. These times were definitely a challenge to get used to since I'm a full-time working professional.

Obstacle after obstacle, and for six months, I overtook each one of them. I had a goal and I achieved it. I am now a proud graduate of Generation USA and have skills in HTML, CSS, Bootstap, and JavaScript. I've worked in a team, I've connected with others with same mentalities. I've grown into someone who prides themselves for being able to learn things that they once believed weren't attainable to them. My goals will only grow, my skills will only grow, and this page is a peep at my progress.
